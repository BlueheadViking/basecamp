Next Generation Job Management System

2025

A mapping-first, mobile-first job management platform built for the next decade of agricultural work—designed to scale, delight, and empower both everyday users and power contractors alike.

Context

Any business trying to get off the ground has to move fast. In those early years, every bit of tech energy is spent shipping features to land the next sale. After multiple pivots, one-offs, and quick fixes, the user experience inevitably starts to show the strain.

That messy, high-velocity phase is vital—and it’s always a little bit thrilling. But eventually, an opportunity comes along to use all that knowledge and redesign for the future: to build the next generation of software that can lead the company for the next 10 years.

When that moment came for our core job management product, I jumped at it. Here’s how I approached rethinking the platform for the modern, mobile, and increasingly tech-savvy agricultural sector.

Key Design Decisions

Drawing on everything I’d learned from years of real-world user feedback, I made several foundational decisions to guide the new system:

  • Mobile-first, mapping-first – The new generation of farmers and contractors are embracing digital tools. By designing for mobile from the start (and mapping as the default), we created an experience that feels at home in the field—and equally powerful on desktop.
  • Fat finger friendly – In every empathy interview, users brought up frustration with cramped UIs. Every tap target, button, and list item was sized for big hands and fast action.
  • Make the simple easy, the complex possible – Most users rely on just a few key workflows. The platform prioritises clarity and intuitiveness for the first 80% of tasks, while still offering deep controls for power users (who, in ag, really love complexity).
  • Build to scale – Scar tissue from years of squeezing features into awkward spaces taught me to always make room for the future. New features and data types have clear, logical places to go—reducing future technical debt.
  • Responsive by default – It’s table stakes, but worth noting: every screen and feature works seamlessly from phone to desktop.

The Rundown

Home View

The new home screen puts actionable data and navigation front and centre. Jobs, maps, and notifications are just a tap away.

Navigation

Intuitive bottom navigation—designed for one-handed use, whether you’re in a truck, on a quad, or at your desk.

Jobs List

Job management at a glance. The list is simple, scannable, and big-finger friendly, but power users can dive into advanced filters and sorting as needed.

Job Summary

Clean summaries present just the right amount of data for quick decisions—without overwhelming users in the field.

Map Interaction

Interactive maps let users instantly jump to jobs, assets, and properties. Designed for clarity in all light conditions, with touch-friendly controls.
